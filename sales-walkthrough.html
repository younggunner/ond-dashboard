The Big Idea

All Sales Are Local

Oil & gas operators are hyper-local. They know who operates on the lease next to theirs. They eat at the same diners. They share the same field offices.

The Cold Call

"Hi, I'm calling from GreaseBook…"

"Not interested." <click>

You told them who you are. Their guard went up. Call's dead.

The Neighbor Call (Pattern Interrupt)

Step 1 — Confirm the property:
"Do you still operate the Lakeside Field in Creek County?"

"Yeah, that's us." (yes-yes)

Step 2 — Build curiosity:
"I ask because we're working with several operators with production right next to yours…"

"Oh really? Like who?" (they're hooked)

You never said who you are or what you sell. They're pulling YOU in.

The Psychology

When you confirm their property first, you get them saying "yes" before their guard is up. They don't know why you're calling — and that curiosity is what keeps them on the line. Once they're hooked, you tiptoe into the connection: "Do you know Acme is operating up there?" Now they're engaged. You never had to pitch. They're asking YOU questions.

Operator Next Door automatically finds every non-client operator who shares a property, a town, or an office building with one of our existing clients — then gives you their contact info, the property name, the county, and the connection story so you can nail this opener every time.

06 — The Plays

Your Playbook

Pick the right play for the situation. Every word is tested. Trust the script.

Play 1: Pattern Interrupt (Cold Outreach)

Use on: Any OND lead you've never spoken to. This is your bread and butter for Silver and Gold leads.

Golden rule: Never reveal who you are or what you sell. Confirm the property first → get them saying yes → build curiosity → tiptoe into the pitch.

Pattern Interrupt — Opener

[Familiar tone — sound like someone they should know. Add natural stutters.]

"Hey, uh, {PROSPECT}, it's… it's {FIRST NAME}. {FIRST LAST} — I'm hoping you can help me out for a moment?"

[Pause — let them respond. Confused tone on next line.]

"If you could clarify something for me… Curious — do you guys still operate the {PROPERTY} in {COUNTY}, {STATE}?"

[Pause — let them confirm. They say "yes." You've got your first yes.]

Pattern Interrupt — Discovery

"I ask because we're working with several operators with production right next to yours, and I was curious — how are you guys managing production updates from your pumpers? Is it text messages, calls, Excel — what's the situation like?"

[Let the prospect talk. LISTEN. Note their exact METHOD.]

"Got it — that makes sense. And how often are they submitting data — once a week, once a month, or does it vary?"

[Listen again. Note exact TIME FRAME wording.]

Pattern Interrupt — Positioning

"That's interesting because most operators are getting their data through {THEIR METHOD} every {THEIR TIME FRAME}, and what they told us was that getting everyone in the operation on the same page was an enormous hassle."

"So what they did was simply have their pumpers download the Greasebook app on their phones and submit all their production updates through it — and not only did they reduce downtime by streamlining communication with the field, they were also able to dial in production and produce more oil because of it."

Pattern Interrupt — Close

"But I'm getting ahead of myself here — Brandon's actually the one who led these projects with those operators. Would you be against him giving you a quick call sometime this week to explain it better?"

"Perfect — he's got some time open this week. He's available {DAY 1} at {TIME 1} or {DAY 2} at {TIME 2} — what works better for you?"

"Ok, and you're on Central Time, right?"

"Awesome — and is {EMAIL ON FILE} still the best email for you?"

[Calendar confirmation:]

"Perfect. You'll see the invite from Brandon hit your inbox in just a minute. Do me a quick favor — open up your email real fast and just make sure you got it. For whatever reason, it hasn't been showing up in people's inboxes lately. You see it there? Great — go ahead and click 'Accept' so we know it went through and you're locked in."

Play 2: Give 1000 Get 1000 (Referral Engine)

Use on: Your existing Greasebook clients who are connected to OND prospects. You're calling INTO your client base — not cold prospects. The dashboard's Referral filter shows you which clients are neighbors to which prospects.

This is the warmest play in the book. Your client already loves the app. Their neighbor is on the OND board. You're handing them $1,000 to make an introduction they'd probably make anyway.

Give 1000 Get 1000 — G2 Check-In

[Start with a genuine check-in. You need to know they're happy before you ask for a favor.]

"Hey {CLIENT}, it's {YOUR NAME} — how's it going?"

[Brief small talk — this is YOUR client, not a cold call. Be real.]

"Hey, real quick — I just wanted to check in. How's the Greasebook been working for you guys? If you had to rate it 1 to 10, what would you say?"

[Listen. Note the score.]

Score 7 or below:

"Totally fair — appreciate you being straight with me. We want you at a 10. Would you be against me having someone from our team reach out to sort that out? They're great — usually get things dialed in real quick."

[Schedule CS callback. Do NOT pitch Give 2000 to an unhappy client. Fix their experience first. Come back later.]

Score 8 or higher — pivot to Give 2000:

"Love hearing that."

Give 1000 Get 1000 — The Referral Ask

[Curious tone — you're asking, not pitching.]

"Hey, so — just curious — have you heard about our Give 1000 Get 1000 program?"

If no (most will say no):

"So basically, if you refer an operator to Greasebook and they move forward, we give them $1,000 to get started — and you get $1,000 in credit on your Greasebook too."

[Pause — let it land. Don't oversell it.]

If yes:

"Oh good — have you used it yet?"

[Listen. If they haven't, continue below.]

Give 1000 Get 1000 — The Name-Drop

[Now use the OND connection. You KNOW the prospect is their neighbor — the dashboard told you.]

"The reason I bring it up — I've actually been trying to get a hold of {PROSPECT} over at {PROSPECT COMPANY}. I think you guys are both on the {PROPERTY} in {COUNTY}?"

[Pause — let them confirm. They WILL know them. That's the whole point of OND.]

"Yeah — so we've been wanting to connect with them. Do you know {PROSPECT NAME} at all?"

[Listen. They'll tell you what they know — this is intel gold.]

If they know them:

"That's what I figured. Would you mind if I mentioned your name when I reach out to them? And honestly, if they end up moving forward, that'd be $1,000 for both of you."

If they offer to make the intro:

"That would be incredible. No pressure at all — even just a heads-up text that I might be calling goes a long way."

[If they agree to intro — get the timing: "When do you think you'd reach out?" Then follow up accordingly.]

If they don't know them well:

"No worries at all. Would you mind if I just mentioned your name when I reach out? Just to say, hey, we work with your neighbor."

Give 1000 Get 1000 — After the Client Call

[Now call the PROSPECT using Play 1 (Pattern Interrupt) — but you've got a warm referral in your back pocket.]

[If the client agreed to make an intro, wait for them to do it first. Then call:]

"{CLIENT} suggested I give you a call. He mentioned you guys are both on {PROPERTY}. I was just curious — how are you guys managing production updates from your pumpers?"

[Then follow the Pattern Interrupt discovery → positioning → close sequence from Play 1.]

Sub-Plays — Situational Tools

These aren't standalone plays — they're tools you'll use inside any play. You'll hit a voicemail, run into a gatekeeper, or have a no-show. Here's how you handle each one.

Voicemail

Use when: You call an OND lead and get their voicemail. Applies to any tier, any play.

Voicemail — Script

[Calm, unhurried, matter-of-fact. No pitch. The curiosity gap does the work.]

"Hey {Prospect}, it's {Your First Name}. I was calling about some of the operators up on {PROPERTY} in {COUNTY}. Give me a call back — {Your phone number}."

[That's it. Hang up. No company name. No product. No "hope to hear from you." The property reference creates the curiosity gap — they'll call back wondering who's doing what on their lease.]

Gatekeeper

Use when: A receptionist or assistant answers. Get through without triggering their "sales call" filter.

Gatekeeper — Script

[Familiar tone — sound like you belong, not like a cold caller.]

"Hey {GATEKEEPER}, this is {YOUR FULL NAME} — I was wondering if you could help me out for a moment…"

[Wait for response]

"Well, I'm looking for {PROSPECT FULL NAME} as I understand he's responsible for the pumpers there — can you put me through?"

Coffee No Show

Use when: A prospect booked a meeting but didn't show. 2-3 reschedule attempts within 5 business days.

Coffee No Show — Script

"Hey {Prospect}? This is {Your Name}, I just had time to get back with you. Hey we spoke — I wanna say a few days ago — about some of the producers we had partnered with next to your production? …and how from my notes it looks like you were communicating with your pumpers via {Method} just like they were — is this ringing any bells?"

[Wait for response]

"Perfect, we'd love to show you how they cut their downtime and got a nice pop in production by streamlining communication with their pumpers — and I just had another slot open up… would you be against scheduling for either {TIME} or {TIME}?"

Handling Scripts — When the Conversation Goes Here

Sometimes a prospect brings up something you need to be trained for. These aren't plays you initiate — they're reactive scripts for when the conversation takes a turn.

"We Already Have SCADA / Telemetry / Remote Monitoring"

Trigger: Prospect says they already have remote monitoring. Don't fight SCADA — position TinyPumper as the faster, cheaper complement.

SCADA Handling — Full Script

"Got it — you're running SCADA. A bunch of folks we work with are in the same spot."

"Mind if I ask you two quick things I've been wondering about?"

"When something breaks, like a tank hits high level or a well stops producing — how do you typically find out? System alert? Pumper call? Next lease run? What's that like?"

[Let them talk. LISTEN. Note HOW they get alerts.]

"Right… And how fast do you usually get that info? Is it real-time, a few hours, or more like the next day?"

[Listen. Note the TIME LAG.]

"It's interesting you say that because most folks are still relying on {THEIR METHOD} and getting it maybe {THEIR TIME FRAME} later. By then the tank's already full or the well's been offline too long."

"So what some of 'em are doing now is plugging in this thing called TinyPumper — installs in 8 minutes, no techs, no wires, no setup fees. Just slap it on and it starts feeding hourly updates straight to your phone or computer."

"It runs on solar with backup — so weather doesn't matter. It just works."

"Compared to SCADA? You catch problems almost in real time — instead of waiting hours or even 'til the next day. And if something breaks, fixing it costs about 5% of what SCADA guys usually pay."

"But I'm getting ahead of myself here — Greg, he is the Founder of TinyPumper and it is already out there, monitoring thousands of assets right now. Would you be against him giving you a quick call sometime this week to explain it better?"

07 — Rebuttals

When They Push Back

These are tested responses. Don't improvise — use the words.

"Not Interested"

"Ohhh yeah and I apologize {Prospect} I REALLY didn't mean to offend you."

[3-second pause]

"And just so you're aware {Prospect} I'm not even sure if we could help you yet. We'd have to understand a bit more about how you're currently managing production and kind of what that looks like because maybe you guys are better off just sticking to what you have now."

"That's why I was curious, you know, how are you guys currently communicating with your pumpers… Is it text, email, Excel, what's the situation like?"

"How Much Does It Cost?"

"Oh yea yea yea, we can go over all the different price points for sure, now just so you're aware {Prospect} we don't have like one product with one price point, it all depends on a few things that we'd have to understand from your side like if you're interested in pumper GPS tracking, an alert engine, a downtime tracker, and things like that, and once we understand all that, Carlos can go through all the different options we have for our operators, would that help you more if we did that?"

"We're Using a Competitor"

"That's actually the reason I'm calling… We have a ton of experience with {Competitor}, I'm curious, what do you like best about their solution?"

[Listen]

"In a perfect world, what would you change?"

[Listen]

"That's interesting because I've talked to dozens of operators who just like you also say {mention their issue or highlight}. And, after seeing the Greasebook we've had half a dozen operators switch in the last few months… and they've all told us the same 3 key reasons for doing so."

"But, {Prospect}, I'm getting ahead of myself here. Brandon is the one who leads this project, and he can walk you through everything. Would you be against him giving you a quick call sometime this week to explain it further?"

"We Have SCADA / Telemetry / Remote Monitoring"

"That's actually the reason I'm calling… Most operators who come to us are also getting their data through {SCADA/Telemetry} — and what they told us was that consolidating all the data from {system} with the updates they get from the Pumpers was creating quite a headache."

"So what they did was simply connect their {system} to the Greasebook AND have their pumpers submit all their production data through the Greasebook app on their phones so everything was in ONE PLACE, and that COMPLETELY streamlined their operations."

"What is Greasebook?"

"That's actually the reason I'm calling… So how are you guys currently getting production updates from the field? Is it texts, phone calls, Excel — what's the setup like?"

[Let them answer. Then:]

"Got it — so {THEIR METHOD}. Most operators we work with were doing the same thing. What they told us was getting everyone on the same page was the hardest part. So they just had their pumpers download an app and submit everything through their phones — gauges, pressures, run tickets, pictures — and it all shows up in real time on a dashboard."

"But I'm getting ahead of myself — would you be against having Brandon walk you through how it actually works?"

"Who Have You Partnered With Near Us?"

"Ah man, we have over 600 operators on the Greasebook at the moment, but Brandon is the one who heads that project, and he can share the list of operators we are working with around you. Can I have him give you a quick follow up call sometime this week to explain it better?"
